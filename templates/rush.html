Rush

Rush is your chance to learn more about Theta Tau. Throughout several events, you’ll get a feel for what our fraternity represents, whether you’re a good fit for us, and whether we’re a good fit for you. At the end of rush, a select number of students will be invited to interviews and may receive bids. Those offered bids can then choose whether they’d like to continue the PNM process.

FAQ

What is Theta Tau?

Theta Tau is a professional engineering fraternity founded in 1904. We are built on the pillars of Brotherhood, Professionalism, and Service, and we bring together engineers who want to grow personally and professionally.

What is Rushing?

Rush is a set of events where students can meet active members, learn about Theta Tau, and see if it’s the right fit. It’s your opportunity to get to know us, and for us to get to know you.

Is there any requirements to rush?

Yes, there is! There's 6 requirements to rush listed below.

  • - Be 18 years of age or older
  • - Purse an ABET Accredited Engineering or Computer Science Major
  • - Have a college GPA (dual enrollment counts)
  • - Submit a rush application before the first day of rush
  • - Go to all events (4/4) with exception of one valid excuse.
  • - Complete a potential new member interview

What happens after rush?

After rush, selected students may be invited to interviews. From there, some will receive bids to become PNMs for Theta Tau.

How is the PNM process?

The PNM process is where new members learn about Theta Tau’s history, values, and traditions. It’s also a time to bond with your PNM class and prepare to become a full member of the fraternity.

Does Theta Tau Haze?

No. Theta Tau does not haze. Hazing is against our values, university policy, and the law. Our PNM process is focused on education, service, and professional growth.

Why should I join Theta Tau?

Joining Theta Tau provides professional networking, leadership development, service opportunities, and lifelong friendships. Members also gain access to a strong alumni network across industries.

How much time does Theta Tau require?

Members usually attend weekly meetings along with professional, service, and social events. The commitment is manageable and designed to complement your academic schedule.

Are there dues?

Yes. Like most fraternities, members pay dues. These support chapter operations, events, and national membership.

Wait, I have more questions!
